Children are our greatest treasure, they are our future

Babies are the embodiment of hope. By saving them, we're not just changing lives; we're shaping a future filled with compassion and promise.

Why the need

abandonment.

An estimated 40 000 babies are abandoned every year in South Africa and we only save about 3000 of these.

Underfunded.

The government facilities to support these babies are very poorly resourced and oversubscribed.

Family-centric.

We believe every child deserves a family that loves them and this is especially important in the formative years.

Gary

Kerry
co-founder of the Upper Highway Baby home. Kerry is mom to all at the home. “The fact the babies born to other women call me mommy is a tragedy and privilege that is never lost on me.”

Becky
Big sister to the little ones and always keeping an eye out for them, Becky knows each baby so well. “Being a big sister to these babies is one of my greatest honours.”

Thobile
Thobi is the director of the Saving Futures Programme. “Hey, meet me, Thobile the baby whisperer. My satisfaction comes from babies happy little faces.”

Precious
Presh is our head carer who helps keeps things running smoothly and the babies happy. Hi, meet Precious, the head of the baby home. I spend hours with the babies making sure they are happy and well cared for.”

Zanele
Zanele is one of our senior day staff. ” Hi, I am Zanele who loves being around the babies. I am loud and funny, and my babies love it!”

Goodman
Goodman is one of drivers for the baby home. ”I am very dedicated to my job and enjoy being around the babies and seeing them being well cared for. My commitment is making sure the baby home is a safe and well cared for environment.”

Bongani
Bongani is our Saving Futures and Upper Highway Baby Home Driver. “Hi this is Bongani. I like to work with people as a team. Working closely with children is especially important to me.”

Ola
